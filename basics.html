Origami Paper

Part of the appeal of origami originates from the ease of obtaining a single sheet of paper at minimum to fold with, whether or not it is a sheet of paper torn from a notebook, printer paper, gift wrapping paper, et cetera. Notably, a thin and square sheet of paper that holds its shape after folding is ideal for most origami projects.

However, if you are looking to specifically purchase origami paper, you can find them at general purpose arts and crafts stores in the U.S. such as Michaels or A.C. Moore.

You can also order online from none other than Amazon or eBay; or, you may order from specialist online stores such as The Origami Paper Shop and Origami Paper Monster.

Diagram Notation

If you have ever looked through the pages of an origami book, or printed out a PDF with instructions for a particular origami model, you may have noticed dotted lines, arrows, and other indicators overlaid over each stage of the model construction process. You can learn to read origami diagrams in full by reading the article provided at Origami Resource Center and other websites. A quick summary of diagram notation is also provided below.

Folds

Valley Fold - Represented by an evenly dashed line and a filled arrow with a black arrowhead. Instructs the folder to fold a section of paper on one side of the line, over the section of paper on the other side of the line.

Mountain Fold - Represented by a repeating dash-dot-dot line and a half-arrow with a white arrowhead. Instructs the folder to fold a section of paper on one side of the line, behind the section of paper on the other side of the line.

In practice, folding a section of paper underneath another section is difficult because you cannot see under the topmost layer of paper. As a result, it is much easier to flip over an origami model, perform a valley fold, and then flip the model back over to complete a single mountain fold.
